In the last few years, the video format is gaining popularity on all platforms. Not just on platforms like YouTube and Vimeo which are primarily meant for video streaming, but also on social media apps like Instagram and LinkedIn.

For our 516 class, we conducted a 2x2 factorial experiment exploring how the presence of people and text affected clicks across categories.

motivation

Increasing points of engagement for content creators

Our study examined how the presence of people or text in thumbnails affects click-through rates across different video categories on YouTube. We analyzed over 10,000 thumbnails and found that the presence of people in thumbnails significantly increased click-through rates across all categories except for gaming. The effect of people in thumbnails varied by category, with food and beauty videos seeing the most significant increase in click-through rates. Our findings have important implications for video creators and marketers looking to optimize their thumbnails to attract more viewers. Further research on thumbnail content and its impact on click-through rates is needed as video content continues to grow in popularity.

Research question

We hypothesiszed that having people or text would affect the click through rate across 4 popular categories on Youtube such as product reviews, music videos, and reaction videos.

  • Hypothesis 1

    Having people on the thumbnail would increase click-through rate.
  • Hypothesis 2

    Having text on the thumbnail would increase click-through rate
Study Design

Participants

Our biggest area of interest were users in the age group 18-34 which is YouTube's largest audience. We also controlled by using frequency of use, and device used for watching YouTube. We controlled for these in order to ensure that we were getting participants that were familiar with the interface and knew how to use the 'categories' portion which we concentrated on. Content categories were chosen before hand by doing some secondary research, and convenience sampling was used in because of the limited amount of time and resources we had for this project.

Study Design

Experiment Design

For our study, we decided to go with a within-subjects because due to the contstraints of time and resources, it required less participants and minimizes random noise.

We controlled variables such as quality of thumbnail used, view counts, and length of video.

2x2 factorial design allowed us to understand the effects of two independent variables (each with two levels) on a single dependent variable.

People No People
Text People + Text No People + Text
No Text People + No Text No People + No Text

There were 72 thumbnails across 3 chosen categories, with each screen randomized for a particular category. Participants went through a screener, then continued on to the test if they qualified. Below is a sample of our mocked up layout.

Why Conditional Logistic Regression?

We decided to use Conditional Logistic Regression to help us analyze two controlled variables and perform stratifying the data bewteen multiple screens. It reduces any sparse data bias that may ovvur when there are several predictor values. We also analyzed the interaction effect between text and people.

We chose not to use ANOVA because it assumes that the data in each category is normally distributed, and because it is not optimal for analyzing ordinal data. Logistic regression makes generalizations based on simple data so it was more relevant for our analysis and our goal of providing recommendations for different video categories on YouTube.

Analyzing data

Results for Product Reviews

Thumbnails with text have 5.24 times better odds of being selected than thumbnails without text (p - 1.39e-11). Thumbnails with people have 3.30 times better odds of being selected than thumbnails without people (p = 2.67e-06). There was a negative interaction when there were both text and people present. Odds of selection were reduced by 0.5 times (p = 0.0262).

Based on the top 3 thumbnails selected, we can see that the text is prominently displayed in the thumbnail with everything else being an accompanying visual which may also explain how thumbnails with text have better odds of being selected and that the interaction of text and people may not matter.

  • Having text has 5.24x better odds than not have text for selection
  • Having people has 3.30x better odds than not having people

Limitations of the Study

As with any other experiment, there are limitations. For future studies, we recommend that participants be screened based on their top 2 devices for watching YouTube. Half of our responses were screened out since they watched more on there smartphone. The study is limited to only two age groups. It would be interesting to study the patters of other age groups as well which could be helpful in guiding platform design. Since we are not experts and not working with Youtube, we recognize that we have limited knowledge of confounding variables that could affect the study.

Our study has low external validity because we chose the thumbnails, and google forms does not closely mimic natural browsing behavior, as well as forcing a selection to be made. It has a high internal validity because we control the selection of thumbnails and what order they are in the screen.

final thoughts

What I learned

  • Cleaning data is HARD WORK!

    I started learning python this quarter for another class, and decided to stick to the language (vs. R) for this quant class. Because we didn't have resources to use MTurk or Qualtrics, we had to clean the google form data and explode the participant rows to 72 per person in order to run the regression properly.

    As the only one with coding knowledge, I took it upon myself to figure out how to use pandas to clean and transform the data (else we were going to go in manually and do it ourselves - only 3k rows of data but still extremely laborious and prone to human errors) and it all worked out in the end. Cleaning the data was 80% of the work. Running the regression was done in a second. It was nice to see significant results.

  • Highly controlled experiments have low external validity

    When we first started out with designing our study, we agreed we wanted to tightly control our variables to avoid any issues with analysis. After this project I understood how there has to be a balance between how controlled a study is and how true to life it could be. Forcing a selection of thumbnails and mocking it up like we did does not mimic natural scrolling behavior. However, I do think we did our best with what resources we had.

  • Experiments are fun!

    I really enjoyed getting our data, having significant results and trying to analyze weird patters in behavior. We used a mixture of quantitative and qualititave questions in our survey which allowed us to have a richer analysis.
