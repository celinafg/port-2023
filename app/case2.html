As fires have become more frequent and intense throughout the western united states, Seattle’s own air quality in October 2022 plunged to an unsettling AQI score of 168, ranking the worst in the world. The salience of this led us to focus on air quality designing under the prompt "how might we do life together" for HCDE 518.

Our proposal is to create a more widespread public health alert system. We explored this solution through the Air Quality System, that helps people find air quality information and masks where they need them.

About KLOA

KLOA is a startup that focuses on making search easier by saving users typing effort. This new search experience consists of an input interface and a contextual search algorithm (see this section for interaction map). Ideally, businesses with mobile shopping apps like Amazon or Instacart would be potential clients of KLOA.

Previous diary studies on KLOA highlighted usability issues that the team is currently iterating on, including what types of feedback to provide users. This research then seeks to understand:

  • 1

    ‍What effect would priming participants have on their experience (i.e. prefacing it as a search experience versus a keyboard)?
  • 2

    How willing participants would be to learn how to use KLOA? How would they prefer to learn about it?
  • 3

    ‍Barriers and frustrations participants encounter when using KLOA in the context of shopping on a mobile app?

KLOA is aware of usability issues and are actively iterating on the design. So our study focused on two hypotheses:

  • Hypothesis 1

    If users don't think of KLOA as a keyboard, they won't try to use it like a QWERTY keyboard
  • Hypothesis 2

    Users will be able to use KLOA if onboarding instructions are provided (a variation of "best case" testing)

There are three components to our setup: camera 1, camera 2, and the prototype. Camera 1 captures participant's hand gesture and movement from above. Camera 2 captures participant's facial reaction and body language. The prototype records the user's interaction with the product.‍‍‍

analysis
and findings

participants felt lost when they didn't get the feedback they were expecting while typing

3 - Critical The lack of feedback while typing resulted in participants expressing doubt and re-doing tasks. 4 out of 5 participants mentioned frustration about not receiving typing feedback (by typing feedback, we are referring to seeing “a” in the search input field after tapping on the key). They struggled while typing because only search suggestions were visible. One participant tried to type the letter ‘b’ but kept getting results starting with ‘c’. The participant tried to long press, double tap, and go into all caps mode, then proceeded to restart the query by deleting input six times.

Participants struggled with the visuals of the input interface‍‍‍

2 - Moderate Most participants struggled with the layout and were unable to understand what the blue keys were and how to use them. They struggled to locate the letters they needed. One participant attempted to search for ‘broccoli’, and was so focused on the keyboard that they didn’t see ‘broccoli’ show up as the top search suggestion. They proceeded to delete/restart the query.‍‍‍

Participants felt frustrated by the experience and preferred QWERTY‍‍‍

3 - Critical Most participants felt the keyboard created a barrier to mobile shopping and struggled to understand what value it brought. 3 out of 5 participants abandoned KLOA and switched back to QWERTY to complete the task. One participant blamed themselves for being unable to type, and found relief when they found the QWERTY keyboard through the red dot.‍‍‍
